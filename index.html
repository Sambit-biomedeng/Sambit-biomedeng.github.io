About Me

I'm a Biomedical Engineer & EEG Research Professional with dual degrees from Jadavpur University and NSEC, combining deep domain expertise in neurophysiological signal processing with hands-on machine learning engineering and industry-academia collaborative research.

My core specialization lies in large-scale EEG data acquisition, spectral and nonlinear analysis, ERP/P300 assessment, and AI-driven validation of neurodiagnostic systems. I've conducted controlled human-subject experiments, acquired RAW EEG data using LabRecorder, and deployed neurotechnology platforms across regulated clinical, investigative, and industrial environments — including forensic neuroscience investigations.

Currently serving as a Junior Research Fellow at NITTTR Kolkata on an Indo-Belgium collaborative project, I build reproducible ML pipelines to detect pathogenic and mutated strains from wastewater metagenomics — translating AI directly into public health surveillance infrastructure.

I'm driven by a single conviction: the right algorithm, applied to the right signal, can surface a clinical truth before symptoms appear.

Metagenomics · Public Health AI
Improved Detection of Pathogenic Strains from Wastewater Metagenomics Data
Problem: Conventional culture-based methods miss novel/mutated pathogenic variants in environmental samples in real time.

Approach: Multi-stage ML pipeline — sequence alignment preprocessing → unsupervised clustering (DBSCAN, k-means) → supervised classification (Random Forest, SVM) to discriminate pathogenic from benign microbial reads.

Outcome: Higher sensitivity for mutated strain detection vs. reference-only approaches across benchmark datasets.
↑ Detection precision on contaminated/mutated sequences — Indo-Belgium collaboration, NITTTR Kolkata
